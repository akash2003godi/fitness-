CARDIO

WHAT IS CARDIO EXACTLY?

Cardio is also known as aerobic exercise which is a physical exercise of low to high intensity that depends primarily depends on the aerobic energy generating process. Cardio training increases the need for blood and nutrients in working muscles and increases the amount of waste products that must be removed from those muscles.

5 CARDIO EXCERCISES WITH BENEFITS OF EACH EXCERCISE

1.WALKING :- To move at a regular pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn, never having both feet off the ground at once

Benefits of Walking

#maintain a healthy weight and lose body fat

#strengthen your bones and muscles

#prevent or manage conditions, such as heart disease, stroke, blood pressure etc..

2.JUMP ROPE:- It is performed by a person where a rope is swung under their feet and over their head

Benefits of Jump Rope

#it helps to tighten muscles

#improve lung capacity

#increases concentration

3.JUMPING JACKS:- Jumping jacks are a beginner cardiovascular exercise that a person can perform almost anywhere

HOW TO PERFORM: Begin with the hip- width apart and arms down. Raise the arms out to the sides, straight in the air while jumping out with the feet apart. jump back into the starting position and land softly on the balls of the feet, bringing the arms back to the body.

Benefits of jumping jacks

#burns more calories

#increases bone density

#improves coordination

4.SQUAT JUMPS:- Squat jumps are a way to increase the exercise intensity while working the glutes, quadriceps, hamstrings, and calves

HOW TO PERFORM: Stand with the feet hip-width apart and arms at the sides, bend the knees to squat, from the squat position, jump in the air and extend the hips until the body is straight , land softly on the balls of the feet, rolling backward to absorb the shock in the heels, repeat using different arm movements to adjust the difficulty.

Benefits of Squat Jumps

#increases your explosive power

#improve upper and lower body strength

#it helps in recovery of hamstrings

5.LATERAL SHUFFLES:- This shuffling motion closely resembles a football or speed-skate warm-up

HOW TO PERFORM: Start on one end of a room and squat slightly, shuffle toward the other side of the room, with the feet meeting together before shuffling out and forward again, pick up speed and increase the exercise intensity

Benefits of lateral jumps

#it increases coordination and balance

#strengthen hip abductors

#it increases heart rate

STRENGTH TRAINING

WHAT EXACTLY IS STRENGTH TRAINING?

Strength Training is also called Resistance training is the use of resistance to muscular contraction to build strength, anaerobic endurance and size of skeletal muscles

5 STRENGTH TRAINING EXERCISES WITH BENEFITS OF EACH EXERCISE

1.LUNGES:- A basic lunge works the muscles in your lower body, including your quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and claves.

HOW TO PERFORM: Stand by standing up tall, feet shoulder-width apart, step forward with your right foot, and lower your hips toward the floor until your right leg is at a 90-degree angle and your left knee is parallel to the ground, make sure your front knee doesn't go beyond your toes, lengthen your spine to keep your torso upright, hold this position for 5 seconds or longer, then step your right foot back to meet your left foot, and repeat this movement with your left leg

Benefits of lunges

#improves spinal health

#gluetal muscle activation

#minimizes risk of injury

2.PLANKS:-Planks are an excellent exercise for improving your core strength and stability. This exercise can also strengthen the muscles in your back, chest, and shoulders

HOW TO PERFORM: Rest on your forearms and toes only, keeping your body in a straight line with your buttocks clenched and your abdominal muscles engaged, Try to hold this position for 30 seconds. If that’s too hard, start with 20 seconds As you gain strength and fitness, try to hold the plank position for 1 minute or longer.

Benefits of planks

#promotes healthy posture

#improves overall mental health

#improves metabolism

3.PUSHUPS:- Standard pushups work the chest muscles (pectorals), as well as the shoulder muscles, triceps, and abdominals.

HOW TO PERFORM: Start in a plank position with your palms directly under your shoulders Keeping your back flat and bracing your core, lower your body by bending your elbows until your chest almost touches the floor, Immediately push your body back up to the starting position, Repeat 8–12 times. Start with 1– 2 sets, and up to 3 sets as you get stronger.

Benefits of push ups

#improves upper-body strength

#improves sports health

#protecting your shoulders and lower back from injuries

4.SQUAT TO OVERHEAD RISE:- If you’re new to strength training, start off by raising your arms overhead without any weight. Once you can do this exercise with good form, you can add light dumbbells, and increase the weight as you build strength

HOW TO PERFORM: Stand with your feet slightly wider than your hips and your arms alongside your body, Slowly lower your hips down into a squat position, Press up to come back into standing and raise your arms overhead, Return to the starting position, Do 1–3 sets of 8–12 repetitions

Benefits of squat to overhead rise

#It increases your overall strength

#activates muscles in upper body

#improves stability

5.WALL ANGELS:- Wall angels are performed by your butt, back, and head against the wall, the slowly gliding your arms up and down the wall in a V to W

benefits of wall angels

#strengthen the rhomboids, and lower and middle traps at the back

#decrease a forward head position

#reduces tension in the upper straps

CORE EXCERCISE

WHAT ACTUALLY ARE CORE EXERCISES?

Any exercise that involves the use of your stomach muscles and back muscles in a coordinated way counts as a core exercise

5 CORE EXERCISES WITH BENEFITS OF EACH EXERCISE

1.CRUNCH:- Crunches are a classic core-strengthening move. The act of lifting your upper body works your abdominal muscles, if you have occasional low back pain, do crunches with care — move slowly and start with just a few reps, If your low back pain is chronic, talk with a certified trainer or healthcare professional before attempting this classic crunch. It may not be the best option for you.

HOW TO PERFORM: Start on your back. Bend your knees and plant your feet on the floor at hip width, Line up your head and spine. Cross your arms across your chest, Tighten your core and relax your neck and shoulders, Tuck in your chin and lift your upper back, keeping your lower back, pelvis, and feet on the floor, Pause Slowly lower your upper back to return to the starting position, Start with 1 set of 8–12 reps.

Benefits of crunches

#helps you build muscles

#strong core

#improves posture

2. BICYCLE CRUNCH:- This Variation on a regular crunch works your obliques, rectus abdomen, and hips

HOW TO PERFORM: With your left knee bent and your right leg straight, lift your right shoulder off the floor and move your right elbow toward your left knee, As you bring your right shoulder back to the floor, extend your left leg while bending your right knee and bringing it toward your chest, As your right knee moves farther in, lift your left shoulder off the floor and move your left elbow toward your right knee, Start with 3 sets of 12 alternate repetitions.

Benefits of Bicycle crunch

#tone the mid section

#improves flexibility

#slims the waist

3. BRIDGE:-This pose activates your glutes to lift your hips, which helps train your core while toning your butt and thighs

HOW TO PERFORM: Start on your back. Bend your knees and plant your feet on the floor at hip width. Place your hands at your sides, palms down ,Tighten your core and glutes, Raise your hips until your knees are in line with your shoulders, Hold for 10–30 seconds, Repeat 3–5 times.

Benefits of Bridge exercise

#reduces knee and back pain

#whittles your waste line

#boosts balance

4. SUPINE TOE TAP:-This is a basic Pilates exercise. It engages your core muscles while working your glutes, hips, and legs. Toe taps also place minimal pressure on your spine. If you have back pain, toe taps may be an ideal alternative to crunches

HOW TO PERFORM: Start on your back. Lift your legs, with your knees bent to 90 degrees. Place your hands at your sides, palms down. Tighten your core. Lower your right foot and gently tap the floor, keeping your left leg still and your back flat. your right leg to return to the starting position. Repeat with your left leg. Start with 1 set of 8–12 reps.

Benefits of Supine toe tap

#strengthens the core and pelvic floor muscles

#acts as gentle support to the lower back

#excess calories are burnt

5.BIRD DOG:- The bird dog engages both your abdominal and back muscles, so it’s an ideal core-strengthening move. It also challenges your coordination, balance, and, stability.

HOW TO PERFORM: Start on all fours, hands below your shoulders and knees below your hips, Tighten your core, Lift and straighten your right leg to hip level. Simultaneously lift and extend your left arm to shoulder level, palm down. Hold a neutral spine without allowing your back to arch as you extend your arm and leg, Pause, Repeat with your left leg and right arm, Start with 1 set of 8–12 reps.

Benefits of Bird Dog

#relieves low back pain

#promotes neutral spine

#improve posture and increase range of motion

MOBILITY EXCERCISE

WHAT ACTUALLY IS MOBILITY EXERCISE?

Mobility exercises are those exercises that make the joints flexible by allowing them to reach their full range of motion. This is done with no stiffness or pain. mobility exercises are the flexibility of muscles to support the joints and the make the tendons more malleable.

5 MOBILITY EXERCISES WITH BENEFITS FOR EACH EXERCISE

1.SPINE WIND MILLS:-Windmill stretch is a great thoracic mobilisation movement. The thoracic spine is the longest part of the spine with 12 vertebra and the ribcage connecting along its length. Windmill Stretch uses breathing and repeated movements to gently restore rotation to this often stiff and neglected area of the spine

HOW TO PERFORM: Start with your feet between hip- and shoulder-width apart Raise one arm overhead, slightly down your other arm to your leg, reverse the movement and start again

Benefits of spine wind mill stretch:-

#allows to work on one side of the body at a time

#the muscles get strong and flexible

#it is a multiplanar exercise which deals with all three planar motions of your body

2.FROG POSE:- Frog Pose, called Mandukasana in Sanskrit, is a yoga pose that targets your core, hips, and inner thighs. It’s sometimes referred to as downward-facing frog, or Adho Mukha Mandukasana, Frog Pose often incorporates breathing and mindfulness practices. It offers many benefits, promoting relaxation and helping release mental and physical tension. The hip-opening posture suits a variety of levels and works wells as part of a Yin, Hatha, or Vinyasa sequence.

HOW TO PERFORM:- Begin on all fours with your hands under your shoulders and your knees beneath your hips, Yield your weight forward onto your hands, Slowly, scoot your knees out to the sides, keeping them in line with your hips, Keep your knees bent and your ankles behind you, in line with your knees, Turn your toes out to the sides, Rest on your forearms with your elbows beneath your shoulders, placing your palms together or on the floor. You can also extend your hands f Forward, resting your chin or forehead on the floor, Sit back into your hips to deepen the pose, Breathe deeply, focusing on relaxing and releasing tension

Benefits of frog pose

#improves hip mobility and eases back pain

#diabetes management

#boosts blood circulation and help manage blood pressure

3.BUTTERFLY STRETCH:-The butterfly stretch is a seated hip opener that has immense benefits and is perfect for all levels, including beginners. It’s effective in relieving tightness in your hips and enhancing flexibility, especially after strenuous workouts, repetitive movements, or prolonged sitting.

HOW TO PERFORM:- Sit on the floor or a prop with the soles of your feet pressing into each other. To deepen the intensity, move your feet closer in toward your hips, Root down into your legs and sitting bones, Elongate and straighten your spine, tucking your chin in toward your chest, With each inhale, lengthen your spine and feel the line of energy extending out through the top of your head, With each exhale, fall heavy into the floor and relax or sink a bit more deeply into the stretch, Hold this position for up to 2 minutes, Repeat 2 to 4 times.

Benefits of butterfly stretch

#recover from athletic activity, such as cycling and running

#improves flexibility

#can help in recovery of injuries of groin and knees

4.HIP OPENER:- This move stretches the muscles in your hip and groin, it also works your core.

HOW TO PERFORM:- Start in push-up position on your hands and toes, Place your right knee near your right elbow, Drop your hips down, Hold for 30 seconds, Return to push-up position. Repeat with the left leg.

Benefits of hip openers:-

#lenghthens multiple muscle groups legs and the groin

#decreases muscle blood flow

#improves ability to do daily activities

5.KNEE TO CHEST STRETCH:- The knee-to-chest stretch focuses on the muscles of your lower back. Avoid this stretch if you have osteoporosis because it may increase the risk of compression fractures in your vertebrae.

HOW TO PERFORM:- Lie on your back on a firm surface with the backs of your heels flat on the floor. Gently pull one knee up to your chest until you feel a stretch in your lower back. Bring the knee as close to your chest as comfortably possible. Keep the opposite leg relaxed in a comfortable position, either with your knee bent or with your leg extended. Hold for about 30 seconds. Switch legs and repeat.

Benefits of knee to chest stretch

#relieves pressure on spinal nerves

#creates more space to nerves to exit from spine

#mainly focuses on lower back
